Overview

Purpose

We love adventure!
Our Purpose here at Dry Oar is to share that same excitement to all the couples out there!
We want all couples, young, or even a little older, to take some needed relax to enjoy this beautiful place with us.

As opposed as our competitors, here at Dry Oar there's No need to bring anything with them, but the desire to learn, if they are new to the sport, or wish to spend a great time to fill out their memory book with their loved one.
We will provide all the equipment needed, fun and entretaiment for a great well spend day!
We also have the best teachers, and staff here at Dry Oar. We want to make sure they get the main idea of why they need to choose Dry Oar over other Rafting places, using a great and attractive design in our website.

Audience

Couples

I would focus on any type of couples, ranging in between their 20's to their 40's, since it's a high risk sport.
But woudn't be limited to that range of age sice we will offer certain sections of the river for beginners. We will focus on couple packages, whose desire is to spend some quality time with the person they love the most.

Why couples?
Rafting is a team work sport, so we would like to encourage couples to work together in order to overcome any rough water they could be facing.

We would like to have an easy access website, easy to check on a smartphone. Most of the couples are busy with work, family, and other stuff, so they won't always have time to sit down and plan a getaway on a desktop.

Typography

Heading Font: Lilita One

So far, I have chosen Lilita One for my headings. Since it is going to be used for the most visible text on the website I wanted it to have some style, and a good kearning. I want to foucus on couples, so I want to include both genders. I think this font is relax, inspired on the outdoor activity and unisex,so it can attract both genders.

Paragraph Font: Gochi Hand

It was easier for me to chose Gochi Hand for the paragraphs of my website than the one for the headings. I tried to use the same context, I wanted a font to be relax and unisex. But also wanted one that could be readable, plus it could actually look diferent, if I decide to add font or cursive if needed in the text.

The Site Map of a site is just like it sounds…it is a map of the pages in a site and how they are related and linked together. From the map above we can see that we will eventually have the Home page and 2 sub or child pages.

The lines that connect them all together indicate that each page should be accessible from any other page, it is essentially showing us the global navigation for the site.

Wireframes

Wireframes are like blueprints for making webpages. They should show the major sections of content that will be on the page and the relative locations of each element. In the wireframe below you can see there will be 6 sections to our page:

  1. At the top we have a section with the logo (the box with the mountain means an image) and the navigation bar.
  2. Then there is a banner image that stretches all the way across the screen.
  3. Next we have some text and an image
  4. ...followed by another row made up of an image and some text.
  5. Then one more section of text with no image.
  6. Lastly, a footer containing a copyright/name line and 3 social media icons.
